History of citizen science

  • The first citizen science initiative was thought to begin in the late 1800s. Wells Cooke, a renowned American ornithologist started collecting data about migratory birds with the help of amateur bird enthusiasts, Later named as North American bird phenology programme. The program lasted for about 90-years and the data collected was on cards and those cards are now being converted into digital format to study the impact of climate change on bird migration.
  • In 1994, the first known publication on citizen science was accomplished by Alan irwin (Irwin et al., 1994). Later on, citizen science gained traction significantly. With an unprecedented number of people having access to the internet and smartphones, Citizen science opens up enormous possibilities to gather large-scale data effectively.

Emerging Citizen science in India

  • Even though India has seen many citizen science programmes in the past, eBird India run by the Bird count India programme become the milestone of Indian citizen science. Its popularity and ease of usage attracted the bird watching community resulting in the growth of CitSci in India. India is a country with high population with most of them having acces to internet and smartphones. This opens up immense potential for the use of Citizen science in research and policy making.
